Council Condemns Antisocial Behaviour

Dec 13, 2024 | News | 0

Hepburn Shire Council is deeply concerned about some recent acts of antisocial behaviour around the Shire.

Hepburn Shire has long prided itself on being a welcoming, inclusive and supportive community that practices respect and celebrates diversity. However, recently Council has been made aware of several appalling incidents that are intending to spread hate and fear.

Mayor, Cr Don Henderson, said that he, the Councillor group and the organisation will not tolerate a small few in the community who attempt to spread misinformation and deface public property with discriminatory language.

“Council stands firm in its commitment to combating hate in all forms. Council will not tolerate homophobia, racism or any behaviour that seeks to diminish and devalue another’s life experience.”

“These recent incidents are against everything this community stands for. We believe in the values of respect, kindness, and equality for all. I want to make it clear that we will not tolerate bigotry. We will not tolerate homophobia. And we will not tolerate racism,” he said.

“We are proud of the diverse, vibrant, and inclusive community we live in. Together, we will continue to stand against hate and discrimination in all forms. We urge anyone witnessing such behavior to report it to the relevant authorities and to stand with those who are affected by it,” said Cr Henderson.

Council encourages anyone who is affected by these recent incidents to reach out to medical professionals for support. For immediate support and online counselling, they should contact:

  • Lifeline website or call 13 11 14, available 24/7
  • Kids Helpline – confidential and anonymous online and phone counselling for people aged 5 to 25

If you have any information about criminal incidents, please contact the Police assistance line on 131 444.

This article is based on a media release from Hepburn Shire.
