Daylesford Arts Autumn Exhibition

Mar 20, 2026 | News | 0

Lauriston Reservoir. Artist Pam Gleeson

Get ready to dive into the vibrant world of art this Easter at Daylesford Arts’ Autumn 2026 Art Exhibition at the Daylesford Town Hall. It’s a cosy, in-person event where you can stroll amongst stunning artworks, meet the artists and soak up some serious creativity vibes. Whether you’re a casual fan or an art aficionado, there’s something here for you. Don’t miss out on this colourful celebration of imagination and talent.

This year we are approaching the Opening Gala Night a little differently. No more lengthy speeches and time-consuming auctions. This year it’s about enjoying yourself, mixing with friends, meeting new people and above all immersing yourself in the creative atmosphere. The Opening Gala Night will be packed with great new art, plenty of good food and drink and you’ll be entertained by the dulcet tones of Cello Connection. This is your chance to purchase an artwork or two before the crowds descend on the exhibition over the long weekend. Though there is no auction this year, there will be several raffles throughout the exhibition with proceeds going to a local charity.

And if that’s not enough to keep you entertained, we are also holding a print-making workshop run by Daylesford artist Sandy Angliss. Attendees will explore a range of simple printmaking techniques in a fun and relaxed atmosphere. All art materials, tea and coffee are provided. Just bring a sandwich and an old shirt or apron.

We have moved our annual exhibition to the Easter long weekend to ease congestion in the local art calendar later in the year and moved the venue to the Town Hall which is once again open to the community.

Venue: Daylesford Town Hall, 76 Vincent Street

Exhibition April 2-7  (10am-4pm)     Free entry

All visitors and lovers of art are welcome.

Opening Gala Night April 3  (5.30pm-8pm)    Tickets $25 * (tickets also available at the door.)

Live music by Cello Connection   *Purchase includes finger food and first drink.
