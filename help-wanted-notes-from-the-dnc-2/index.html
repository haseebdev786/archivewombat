Plants Please! Donations Needed for The Paddock Project

Daylesford Neighbourhood Centre is calling on the community to help bring The Paddock Project to life by donating seedlings, cuttings, and established plants.

The Paddock Project is a bold, community-led initiative transforming unused outdoor space into a thriving green hub – featuring fruit trees, veggie gardens, Indigenous edible plants, a chook run, worm farm and a permaculture-inspired food forest.

We’re seeking donations of:

  • Seedlings or cuttings of herbs, vegetables, fruit trees, or flowers
  • Indigenous edible or medicinal plants
  • Healthy established plants that could be part of a productive or biodiverse garden.

Whether you have a few spare seedlings or a thriving plant that needs a new home, your contribution will help support local food security, sustainability and community connection.

Donations can be dropped off at Daylesford Neighbourhood Centre during office hours of Mon-Thurs 9am-3pm

For large donations or special plant varieties, please contact us to arrange a time.

Let’s grow something great together!

Say Cheese! Cheesemaking Workshop with Barry

Have you ever dreamed of crafting your own delicious cheese at home? Now’s your chance to turn that dream into a delicious reality! Daylesford Neighbourhood Centre is thrilled to welcome Barry the Cheeseman for a fun, hands-on workshop that’s as informative as it is tasty.

Join us on Saturday 25 October 2025 for Say Cheese! Learn the Art of Cheesemaking with Barry – a unique opportunity to dive into the world of DIY cheesemaking with a passionate expert by your side.  Whether you’re a curious beginner, a dedicated foodie, or just keen to try something new, this workshop promises to be a highlight on the calendar.

To make sure everyone gets a chance to join the fun, we’re offering two separate workshop sessions:

Morning Workshop: 10am – 2pm

or

Afternoon Workshop: 4pm – 7pm

What’s included:

  • All ingredients and equipment supplied;
  • Step-by-step cheesemaking guidance from Barry;
  • Take-home cheese, recipe notes & newfound skills;
  • A hearty soup and cheese toastie; and
  • Plenty of laughs and great company

No fancy kitchen required to keep crafting at home.  Hosted by Daylesford Neighbourhood House, this relaxed, beginner-friendly workshop is an experience not to be missed.  Come solo or bring a friend – either way, you’ll leave with new knowledge, new connections and your very own handmade cheese.

Spots are limited and filling fast – bookings essential.

Reserve your place today at www.dncentre.org.au

Question or pondering?  Contact us admin@dncentre.org.au or by phone on 5348 3569

DNC Joke of The Week

A taxi passenger tapped the driver on the shoulder to ask him a question.

The driver screamed, lost control of the car, nearly hit a bus, went up on the footpath, and stopped inches from a shop window.

For a second, everything was quiet in the cab. Then the driver said, “Look, mate, don’t ever do that again. You scared the living daylights out of me!”

The passenger apologized and said, “I didn’t realize that a little tap would scare you so much.”

The driver replied, “Sorry, it’s not really your fault. Today is my first day as a cab driver — I’ve been driving a funeral van for the last 25 years.”
