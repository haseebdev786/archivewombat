Historic Hepburn Bathhouse Lease Renewed

Sep 13, 2024 | News, Living well | 0

Hepburn Shire Council has renewed the lease to its current tenant at the historic Hepburn Bathhouse & Spa.

Belgravia Leisure has managed the premises for the past 16 years and looks forward to continuing at this location.

Mayor, Cr Brian Hood, said the Hepburn Bathhouse & Spa is a significant asset which sits within the Hepburn Mineral Springs Reserve.

“The premises underwent significant renovations in 2008, which were carried out by the State Government. It is now time for a rejuvenation, with a $2 million investment from Belgravia Leisure for capital works improvements. This investment is vital to sustain the asset. Alongside that, Council will invest in some structural and drainage works to maintain the site’s longevity.”

Many visitors come to Hepburn to take advantage of the mineral waters that are drawn directly from the source and used in the Bathhouse. In the competitive spa and wellness space, the upgraded facilities are well-placed to maintain the Bathhouse’s excellent reputation and continue to be a high-quality destination drawcard for our region.

The venue provides local services and products to enhance the visitor experience. There is also a social community benefit through the local community pricing regime for access to the Bathhouse.

“We are pleased to have the support of the Department of Energy, Environment and Climate Action, and Regional Development Victoria for this new lease. The interior upgrades will continue a bathing tradition that began in 1895, and prior to that, a place of healing and wellness for the local Indigenous population.”

Nick Cox, CEO at Belgravia Leisure said, “We are honoured to continue our management tenure at Hepburn Bathhouse and Spa and appreciate that with this privilege comes great responsibility. We will work hard to continue to preserve the Bathhouse’s exceptional reputation and attract locals and tourists alike to the area, enabling the community to become a self-sustaining travel destination”.

Cox continued; “We have also committed to a $2 million investment to ensure the longevity of this historic gem. The investment will enable amenity upgrades which includes the changing facilities as well as the introduction of new experiences that will improve our wellness offerings and attract new markets.

Environmental sustainability initiatives also form part of the capital program, including more sustainable plant and equipment.

The Hepburn Bathhouse & Spa is open every day except Christmas Day.

This article is based on a media release from Hepburn Shire Council.
