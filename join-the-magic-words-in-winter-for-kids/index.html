Join the Magic: Words in Winter FOR KIDS!

Aug 15, 2025 | Living well | 0

If you were thinking that Words in Winter was just for adults, think again! The festival is far more than an adults-only affair. The program this year is bursting with events designed especially for children, teens, and families, offering comics workshops, musical storytelling, monsters and chances for young people to share their own voices. There’s something to spark every imagination.

Storytime at the Library

A warm and enchanting storytime featuring local authors Kate Foulds and Claire Clifton, along with visiting author Julie Murphy. Delight in imaginative tales—perfect for little readers and families alike.

When: Saturday, 23 August, 10 am – 12 pm
Where: Daylesford Library
Bookings: Storytime at the Library on Humanitix

School Writing Awards — with guest author Emma Gourlay & Ruby West-Dawson

A celebration of young writers from Yandoit, Daylesford, St Michaels, Dharma, Creswick, and Trentham primary schools. Guest author Emma Gourlay will present awards and engage in conversation with Ruby West-Dawson (they/them), a young person from Paradise Bookshop. It’s a proud moment for the whole community—cheer on local talent and enjoy the thrill of stories brought to life.

When: Sunday, 24 August, 10:00 am to 11:00 am
Where: Radius Art
Free Event. Register: School Writing Awards with Guest Author Emma Gourlay

Comics Workshop — “Squishbook: Make Comics”

A fun and creative workshop led by Squishface Studio and friends for budding comic-book artists and writers. All materials included—just bring your imagination! Perfect for kids aged 8 and up looking to invent characters, panels, and storylines.

When: Saturday, 23 August, 1:30 pm to 3:00 pm
Where: Paradise Bookshop
Free Event: Tickets: Squishbook – Make Comics with Squishface Studio and Friends

Creative Writing Workshop — “Making a Monster”

A teen-friendly literary adventure! Dive into the world of monsters—from mythic beasts to modern-day imaginaries—and explore themes of fear, bravery, and creative expression with guidance from the instructor. For ages 12+, perfect for writers ready to craft their own legendary (or hilarious) creatures.

When: Sunday, August 24, 1:30 pm to 3:00 pm
Where: Paradise Bookshop
Tickets $20: Making a Monster workshop

All-Ages Finale — Family Farm Band: I Saw the Light

A rollicking, musical storytelling performance by the Family Farm Band—celebrating their 20-year milestone. Music, stories, and family fun await in this uplifting highlight of the festival.

When: Sunday, 31 August, 2:00 pm to 4:00 pm
Where: Radius Art
Tickets $10: I Saw the Light: Southern Gothic Musical Storytelling with Family Farm Band

Words in Winter spans two cosy weekends—Friday–Sunday, 22–24 August and repeat magic on Friday–Sunday, 29–31 August—all across venues nestled in beautiful Daylesford and Hepburn Springs on Djaara country. See the full program at wordsinwinter.com
