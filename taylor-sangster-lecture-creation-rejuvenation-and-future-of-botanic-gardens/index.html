Taylor & Sangster Lecture: Creation, Rejuvenation and Future of Botanic Gardens

Jul 18, 2025 | Features, Living well | 0

John Hawker

The Friends of Wombat Hill Botanic Gardens invite community members and garden enthusiasts to a special Taylor & Sangster Lecture on Thursday 7 August at 5.30pm, to be held in Stanbridge Hall.

In 1884 the Daylesford Borough Council commissioned the eminent nursery men and  landscape designers, William Taylor and William Sangster, to lay out the Public Gardens. The Friends of Wombat Hill Botanical Gardens celebrate their contribution to the Gardens with an occasional lecture series in their name.

This year’s lecture will feature John Hawker, renowned horticulturist and a long-time friend of the Gardens and the Friends group. John’s deep knowledge of historic and botanic gardens—along with his warm connection to Wombat Hill—will make this a memorable evening.

In 2022, John was part of a specialist team commissioned by Hepburn Shire to complete a Tree Collections Report for the Wombat Hill Botanic Gardens. His lecture is expected to draw on insights from that work, offering reflections on the Gardens’ significant trees and their ongoing conservation.

John Hawker brings over four decades of experience in horticulture, botany and garden heritage. With qualifications in Agricultural Science, Horticultural Science and Viticulture, John has worked at the Royal Botanic Gardens Melbourne, Urban Design, and Heritage Victoria, where he served for 23 years assessing and advising on heritage places. He is also a Churchill Fellow, co-author of Elms in Australia, and has played advisory roles with a range of significant garden and heritage organisations.

In 2017, John visited the Wombat Hill Botanic Gardens as Heritage Officer (Horticulture) with Heritage Victoria, contributing to important discussions about heritage management and the Gardens’ Master Plan.

This event is expected to draw strong interest—please RSVP by 1 August to Friends Secretary Denise Robinson at lake.dc43@gmail.com.

All are welcome to attend what promises to be an informative and inspiring evening celebrating the heritage and future of our beloved Gardens.
